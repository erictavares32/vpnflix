Privacy & Anonymity

Mullvad is widely regarded as the most private VPN on the market. It allows account creation without any personal info — no email, no name, just a random account number. You can pay with cash mailed in an envelope or various cryptocurrencies.

ProtonVPN also maintains strict no-logs policies and uses Swiss privacy laws to protect user data. However, account creation requires an email address and doesn't allow for truly anonymous payments outside Bitcoin.

Security Features

  • Both use AES-256 encryption
  • Mullvad supports WireGuard and OpenVPN protocols
  • ProtonVPN includes Secure Core (multi-hop routing)
  • Both VPNs run their infrastructure without hard drives (RAM-only or similar)

Streaming & Unblocking

Mullvad has a strict focus on privacy and makes no effort to unblock streaming services like Netflix or BBC iPlayer.

ProtonVPN, on the other hand, offers a premium-tier service specifically designed to unblock Netflix, Disney+, Hulu, and more. It’s a better choice for users who prioritize both privacy and streaming.

Speed & Performance

Both VPNs are fast, especially over WireGuard. In speed tests:

  • Mullvad: 820–870 Mbps
  • ProtonVPN: 850–900 Mbps (on premium servers)

Both are excellent for general browsing, P2P, and secure downloads.

App Design & Usability

ProtonVPN offers sleek, easy-to-use apps for Windows, macOS, Android, iOS, and Linux. Mullvad apps are simple and secure, but minimal.

Pricing Comparison (2025)

  • Mullvad: €5/month flat — no long-term discounts
  • ProtonVPN: Free tier available; paid plans start at €4.99/mo (2-year plan)

Who Should Use Which?

  • Choose Mullvad if you want maximum anonymity and simplicity.
  • Choose ProtonVPN if you need more features, streaming support, and cross-platform apps.
